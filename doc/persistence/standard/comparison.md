# Comparison: Standard vs Cohomology Reduction

Standard homology reduction and cohomology reduction differ in several key aspects. Standard reduction operates on the boundary matrix and processes columns left-to-right (increasing filtration), while cohomology operates on the coboundary matrix and processes right-to-left (decreasing filtration). Both start with low initial column density -- for standard reduction, d-simplices have d+1 non-zeros, while cohomology starts with low coface counts in higher dimensions. However, columns in standard reduction get denser over time, whereas cohomology columns stay sparse. Standard reduction finds pivots by scanning from the bottom, while cohomology can scan from either direction depending on convention. Clearing in standard reduction eliminates birth columns, while cohomology clearing eliminates death columns as a dual operation. Standard reduction is best suited for dense filtrations and small complexes, whereas cohomology excels on sparse filtrations and large complexes. Standard reduction maps poorly to GPUs due to sequential dependencies, while cohomology's column-independent operations map naturally. SIMD benefit is moderate for standard reduction and high for cohomology. In terms of typical speed, standard reduction serves as the baseline, while cohomology achieves 2-10x speedup on sparse data.
